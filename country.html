Deepawali is a Sanskrit word, which is made up of two words, Deep and Awali. Deep means light and Awali mean row or line. In this way, Deepawali means a row of lights. Deepawali is also called the festival of light and Deepotsav. This special festival celebrated on the new moon day of Kartik month teaches people to move from darkness towards light. This festival is celebrated not only celebrated by the Hindu, but also by Sikhs, Buddhist and Jainism with their own beliefs. This holy festival has different importance in different religions.

Importance of Diwali

The festival of Diwali is celebrated by the people with great fun and lots of fun activities, it becomes the happiest holiday for the Indian people in the year and is celebrated with significant preparation. It is a festival of high importance to the Indian people, during which people clean their homes, do decorations, do shopping, buy many new things including gifts, kitchen utensils, appliances, cars, golden ornaments, etc. and performs a lot of rituals. There are many ancient legends and myths about celebrating this festival. Girls make rangoli in creative patterns on the floor near the doors and walkways of the house. There are very few variations in the celebration of this festival according to regional practices and rituals. The spiritual significance of this festival signifies the victory of light over darkness and the victory of good over evil. It is celebrated to honour Goddess of Wealth, Lakshmi and God of Wisdom, Ganesh. Its religious significance varies from region to region across the country. Somewhere it is celebrated to honour the return of Rama, Sita and Lakshmana to their home after a long exile period of 14 years (according to the Hindu epic Ramayana). Some people celebrate it to remember the return of the Pandavas to their kingdom after 12 years of exile and one year of Agyatvas (according to the Hindu Epic Mahabharata). It is also believed that it began to be celebrated when Goddess Lakshmi was born after churning the sea by gods and demons. The Diwali festival marks the beginning of a new Hindu year in the west and some northern parts of India. It is celebrated as Bandi Chhor Divas by burning the Golden Temple by the people of Sikhism. It is celebrated by people of Jainism to mark the nirvana achieved from Shikharji by Mahavira.
